A* Algorithm

One major practical drawback is its O ( b d ) {\displaystyle O(b^{d})} space complexity where d is the depth of the solution (the length of the shortest path) and b is the branching factor (the average number of successors per state), as it stores all generated nodes in memory. Thus, in practical travel-routing systems, it is generally outperformed by algorithms that can pre-process the graph to attain better performance,[2] as well as by memory-bounded approaches; however, A* is still the best solution in many cases.[3]
